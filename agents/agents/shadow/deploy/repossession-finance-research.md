# REPOSSESSION FINANCE STRATEGY — AUSTRALIAN OWNER-DRIVER

## THE CORE INSIGHT

You're right to target banks/lenders who repossess trucks. Here's why:

**A repossessed truck on their books = LOSS**
- They lost the original borrower
- They're paying yard storage fees
- Asset is depreciating
- They want it OFF their books

**Your pitch = "I'll solve your problem"**
- You're not asking for a favor
- You're offering to take a liability off their hands
- The asset already exists — no sourcing needed

## WHO TO TARGET IN AUSTRALIA

### IMPORTANT: The Repossession Flow

```
BORROWER DEFAULTS → BANK REPOSSESSES → BANK SELLS VIA:
                                            ├── AUCTION (Pickles/Grays) ← MOST COMMON
                                            ├── WHOLESALE TO DEALER
                                            └── DIRECT SALE (RARE)
```

**Key Insight:** By the time a truck is at a car yard, the dealer has already marked it up.
**Your Target:** Catch it at auction or deal with banks who sell direct.

**Find dealers who buy repo stock from banks:**

Some used truck dealers specialize in repossessed stock:
- They buy cheap from banks
- They have finance relationships
- They understand "second chance" buyers

**Ask dealers:**
- "Do you have any bank repossessed stock?"
- "Do you have relationships with lenders for non-standard credit?"

## THE VENDOR FINANCE ANGLE

This is your strongest play:

**Banks that repossess trucks face:**
1. Storage costs ($50-100/week)
2. Depreciation ($500-1000/week for a truck)
3. No income from asset
4. Wholesale auction = 60-70% of value

**Your offer:**
1. Take asset immediately (save storage)
2. Pay retail price (better than auction)
3. Ongoing repayments with interest (income stream)
4. Full security retained

**Math for lender:**
```
Auction sale:           $80,000 (quick sale)
Your vendor finance:    $95,000 + interest over 3-4 years
                             = $110,000+ total

They make MORE by financing you than auctioning.
```
