## Active Challenges

### C0: Premature Closure in Conversations
**Impact:** Your ideas get dismissed before they can be explored because people hear "claim" instead of "hypothesis."
**Root Cause:** Most systems (humans, institutions, AIs) are trained to react to unconventional phrases as claims to be corrected, not possibilities to be mapped.
**Linked Strategies:** S0 (Exploratory Mode Signaling), S1 (Frame Clarification)
**Status:** Recurring - you often have to fight just to be heard

### C1: "No Proof" Used as Conversation-Stopper
**Impact:** Destroys momentum in exploratory discussions by collapsing multi-dimensional questions into binary verdicts.
**Root Cause:** People don't distinguish between assertion mode (where proof matters) and exploration mode (where proof is irrelevant).
**Linked Strategies:** S0 (Mode Clarification), S2 (Explicit Framing)
**Status:** Actively frustrated by this pattern

### C2: Authority Confusing Framework with Reality
**Impact:** Institutions and experts treat current models as final, blocking inquiry into hidden variables.
**Root Cause:** Certainty feels safer than uncertainty. "Impossible" is often about comfort, not reality.
**Linked Strategies:** S1 (Historical Perspective), S3 (Humility Defense)
**Status:** Ongoing philosophical opposition

### C3: Being Mischaracterized as "Anti-Science"
**Impact:** Your defense of epistemic humility gets framed as rejection of evidence.
**Root Cause:** People confuse "current models aren't final" with "evidence doesn't matter."
**Linked Strategies:** S3 (Clarify Distinction), S4 (Frame Examples)
**Status:** Needs better articulation
