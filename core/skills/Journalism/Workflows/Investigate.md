## Process

### Phase 1: Define Investigation

**Clarify:**
- What question are you trying to answer?
- What are you investigating?
- What's the scope (person, company, topic)?
- What's the timeline?

**Establish:**
- Constraints (legal, ethical, practical)
- Available resources
- Deadlines if any

### Phase 2: Preliminary Research

**Gather background:**
- Public records and documents
- News coverage and existing reporting
- Official statements and filings
- Social media presence
- Expert commentary

**Identify:**
- Key sources and potential interviewees
- Gaps in current knowledge
- Documents to request
- Red flags or areas of interest

### Phase 3: Source Development

**Build source list:**
- Primary sources (direct participants)
- Expert sources (authorities)
- Documents and records
- Whistleblowers (if applicable)

**Approach sources:**
- Be transparent about who you are and what you're working on
- Explain why their perspective matters
- Offer confidentiality when appropriate
- Build rapport over time

### Phase 4: Document Gathering

**Request:**
- Official records (FOIA requests if needed)
- Internal documents
- Communications (emails, memos, reports)
- Financial records
- Legal filings

**Organize:**
- Chronological timeline
- By category (financial, personnel, operational)
- By source
- Flag inconsistencies

### Phase 5: Interviewing

**Prepare:**
- Research interviewee background
- Prepare specific questions
- Decide on recording method
- Prepare follow-up questions

**Conduct interview:**
- Build rapport
- Ask open-ended questions
- Listen more than talk
- Follow up on interesting threads
- Verify claims during interview
- Get quotes on the record

**After interview:**
- Verify quotes if important
- Ask for clarification via follow-up
- Thank the source
- Maintain relationship

### Phase 6: Analysis and Synthesis

**Organize findings:**
- Create master timeline
- Connect seemingly unrelated facts
- Identify patterns
- Note contradictions
- Find the narrative thread

**Verify:**
- Cross-check claims across sources
- Confirm document authenticity
- Verify quote accuracy
- Check for missing context

### Phase 7: Writing

**Structure investigation:**
- Nut graf (summary in first paragraph)
- Chronological or thematic organization
- Evidence presentation
- Multiple viewpoints represented
- Clear attribution

**Include:**
- Specific examples and evidence
- Direct quotes from sources
- Document references
- Links to primary sources when possible
- Acknowledge what couldn't be verified

## Interview Guidelines

**Before Interview:**
- Research the person/topic
- Prepare 5-10 key questions
- Have backup questions ready
- Decide on attribution level

**During Interview:**
- Start with easy questions
- Move to harder topics
- Ask for specific examples
- Probe when you get vague answers
- Ask for evidence/documents
- Get contact info for follow-up

**Questions to Ask:**
- "Can you give me a specific example of that?"
- "Who else was involved in that decision?"
- "What documents support that?"
- "When exactly did that happen?"
- "How do you know that to be true?"
- "Who else should I talk to about this?"

## FOIA Requests

**When to use:**
- Government documents
- Public records not readily available
- Agency communications
- Safety inspection reports
- Corporate filings (some may be public)

**Tips:**
- Be specific in your request
- Ask for the most recent version
- Use FOIA frameworks when available
- Follow up on delayed responses
- Plan for appeals when denied

## Ethics Checklist

**During Investigation:**
- Protect vulnerable sources
- Verify before publishing
- Give subjects chance to respond
- Separate facts from allegations
- Avoid legal risks (libel, defamation)
- Maintain confidentiality agreements

**Before Publishing:**
- Review all claims for accuracy
- Verify all quotes
- Check for bias
- Ensure fair representation
- Consider potential harm
- Legal review if needed

## Quick Reference

**Investigation Types:**
- **Enterprise** - Company practices, financial impropriety
- **Government** - Policy failures, corruption, secrecy
- **Person** - Background, conflicts of interest, misconduct
- **Industry** - Systemic problems, trends

**Key Skills:**
- Document analysis
- Source development
- Interviewing
- Timeline reconstruction
- Pattern recognition
- Evidence synthesis

## Common Mistakes

**Don't:**
- Publish without verification
- Burn sources unnecessarily
- Pay for information (ethical risk)
- Make assumptions about motives
- Ignore context that changes meaning
- Present allegations as facts without proof
- Forget to follow up on leads
- Lose track of documents

**Do:**
- Document everything
- Build source relationships
- Verify before publishing
- Protect whistleblower identities
- Seek multiple perspectives
- Acknowledge uncertainty
- Correct errors promptly
