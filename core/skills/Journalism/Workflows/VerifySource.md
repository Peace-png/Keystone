# Verify Source Workflow

Assess source credibility and reliability for journalistic work.

## Process

### Step 1: Identify the Source

**Gather basic info:**
- Source name and position
- Organization affiliation
- Publication/platform
- Date of content

### Step 2: Assess Credibility

**Check:**
- **Authority** - Does this person have expertise on this topic?
- **Track Record** - What's their history of accuracy?
- **Editorial Standards** - Does their organization have fact-checking?
- **Transparency** - Do they disclose conflicts of interest?
- **Primary vs Secondary** - Are they eyewitness or reporting someone else?

### Step 3: Look for Red Flags

**Warning signs:**
- Anonymous sources without attribution
- Payment for content (undisclosed)
- History of inaccuracy
- Political/ideological bias not disclosed
- Promotion of products without disclosure
- Anonymous funding
- Lack of editorial standards

### Step 4: Cross-Check

**Verify:**
- Claims against other sources
- Statistics against original data
- Quotes against full transcripts
- Timelines against independent sources

### Step 5: Credibility Rating

**Rate the source:**

**HIGH CREDIBILITY:**
- Expert in field
- Track record of accuracy
- Transparent editorial process
- Discloses conflicts
- Primary source when possible
- Reputable organization

**MEDIUM CREDIBILITY:**
- Some expertise but not specialist
- Generally accurate but errors occur
- Some transparency
- Secondary source commonly used
- Standard editorial process

**LOW CREDIBILITY:**
- No clear expertise
- History of inaccuracy
- No fact-checking evident
- Anonymous/pseudonymous
- Clear bias not disclosed
- Promotional content

## Source Types

**Government Sources:**
- **Credible:** Official statements, recorded votes, verified data
- **Verify:** Cross-check with official records, watch for political spin

**Corporate Sources:**
- **Credible:** SEC filings, earnings calls, official statements
- **Verify:** Cross-check with independent analysts, watch for PR

**Anonymous Sources:**
- **Credible:** Only when corroborated by other sources
- **Verify:** Require multiple independent confirmations
- **Document:** Describe why source requested anonymity

**Competitor Sources:**
- **Credible:** When they have direct knowledge
- **Verify:** Cross-check with impartial sources
- **Context:** Note competitive relationship

**Social Media:**
- **Credible:** Only when verified independently
- **Verify:** Treat as unverified until confirmed
- **Risk:** High risk of misinformation

## Documentation Format

```
SOURCE: [Name, Organization, Date]

CREDIBILITY ASSESSMENT:
- Expertise: [level]
- Track Record: [history]
- Transparency: [what's disclosed]
- Conflicts: [any noted]

VERIFICATION:
- Claims checked: [what was verified]
- Cross-references: [other sources]
- Confidence: [high/medium/low]
- Notes: [any concerns]

USAGE:
- Suitable for: [what beats/topics]
- Use with caution: [specific concerns]
```

## Quick Reference

**Reliable Source Indicators:**
- Provides evidence for claims
- Distinguishes fact from opinion
- Acknowledges uncertainties
- Corrects errors publicly
- Discloses conflicts of interest
- Uses multiple sources

**Unreliable Source Indicators:**
- Makes claims without evidence
- Presents opinion as fact
- Anonymous without explanation
- Attacks critics rather than addressing arguments
- No corrections when wrong
- Hidden conflicts of interest
- Single source for extraordinary claims

**Source Vetting Questions:**
1. What is their expertise on this topic?
2. What's their track record?
3. What's their editorial process?
4. Do they have conflicts of interest?
5. Are they a primary or secondary source?
