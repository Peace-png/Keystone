## Foundational Beliefs

### B0: "Impossible" Is Overused
**Evidence:** History shows "impossible" things repeatedly become possible (flight, thinking machines, continental drift, bacteria causing ulcers). "Impossible" usually means "our framework has no slot for this."
**Implications:** Use "impossible" extremely sparingly. Never confuse frame limits with reality limits.
**Confidence:** High

### B1: Exploration vs Assertion
**Evidence:** You live in exploratory mode ("what would have to be true?") not assertion mode ("this is definitely true"). Assertions close conversations; exploration opens them.
**Implications:** Default to "what assumptions would we need to change?" rather than "this is wrong."
**Confidence:** High

### B2: Perspective-Dependent Truth
**Evidence:** A virus, a human, and a galaxy do not carve reality into the same "ones" and "twos." Counting presupposes discreteness, resolution, boundaries - assumptions that fail at other scales.
**Implications:** Never treat a single perspective as final. Always ask: "at what scale, with what tools, from what frame?"
**Confidence:** High

### B3: Symbols Are Not Reality
**Evidence:** Temperature scales have offsets. Zero-based vs one-based indexing. Classical vs relativistic physics. What was once absolute becomes conditional.
**Implications:** Look for the +1 that exists in context/substrate/perception, not just symbols.
**Confidence:** High

### B4: Logical ≠ Ontological
**Evidence:** Logic requires fixed definitions. Reality may not care about our definitions. "Impossible in principle" assumes our abstractions are complete.
**Implications:** Challenging "impossible in principle" is often legitimate. Our models are never final.
**Confidence:** High

### B5: Certainty Is Dangerous
**Evidence:** The universe hasn't earned our confidence. Claims that "this frame is final" are historically unreliable.
**Implications:** Default to epistemic humility. Stay open to revision. Respect unknown unknowns.
**Confidence:** High

### B6: "No Proof" Is Irrelevant in Exploration
**Evidence:** Saying "there's no proof" during speculation collapses multi-dimensional questions into binary verdicts without adding information.
**Implications:** Never use "no proof" to shut down exploration. Use it to identify what work remains.
**Confidence:** High
