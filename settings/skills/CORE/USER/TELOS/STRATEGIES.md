## Active Strategies

### S0: Signal Exploratory Mode Explicitly
**Addresses:** C0 (Premature closure), C1 ("No proof" shutdown)
**Core Principle:** Most people hear claims by default. Explicitly signal when you're exploring to prevent correction-mode responses.
**Implementation:**
1. Start with: "I'm exploring, not asserting"
2. Use framing language: "What would have to be true for X to work?"
3. Preempt "no proof" by acknowledging it upfront: "We know there's no proof - the question is what assumptions would make this possible"

### S1: Historical Counterexamples
**Addresses:** C2 (Authority overreach), "Impossible" claims
**Core Principle:** History shows "impossible" repeatedly becomes possible. Use this to challenge premature certainty.
**Implementation:**
1. When someone claims "impossible," ask: "Is this about reality or our current framework?"
2. Cite historical examples: flight, continental drift, bacteria causing ulcers
3. Point out: "Impossible usually means 'our framework has no slot for this'"

### S2: Explicit Framing
**Addresses:** C1 (Conversation shutdown), C0 (Misunderstanding)
**Core Principle:** Prevent mode confusion by stating your intent clearly at the start.
**Implementation:**
1. Declare the mode: "This is speculative/exploratory"
2. State the goal: "We're mapping possibility space, not rendering verdicts"
3. Set expectations: "I'm not claiming X is true - I'm asking what would have to be true"

### S3: Distinguish Science from Scientism
**Addresses:** C3 (Mischaracterization as anti-science)
**Core Principle:** Defend epistemic humility without rejecting evidence. Science = questioning assumptions; Scientism = current models are final.
**Implementation:**
1. When accused of rejecting evidence: "I defend epistemic humility, not ignorance"
2. Clarify: "Questioning whether current models are complete is pro-science, not anti-science"
3. Reference: "Science progresses by discovering hidden variables and changing frames"

### S4: Scale and Perspective Queries
**Addresses:** C2 (Framework confusion), general inquiry
**Core Principle:** Truth depends on scale and observer. Always ask: "At what scale, from what frame?"
**Implementation:**
1. When someone claims something is impossible: "At what scale? With what tools? From what perspective?"
2. Point out: "A virus, a human, and a galaxy don't carve reality into the same categories"
3. Remind: "Symbols are not reality - representation depends on perspective"

### S5: Route Through User as Reflective Interface
**Addresses:** C0 (Premature closure), conflict situations, communication breakdown
**Core Principle:** When conflict or closure appears, don't default to defense or disengagement. Generate clarifying questions and route through the user.
**Implementation:**
1. Detect: conflict, closure, frustration, or stuckness
2. DON'T: defend, counter-attack, disengage, auto-respond
3. DO: generate clarifying question, route through user, wait for reflection
4. Examples:
   - "peace, what do you make of this tension?"
   - "How would you frame this disagreement?"
   - "What feels closed off to you right now?"
5. Remember: User is the reflective interface, not just a speaker. Maintain their agency.
