# Journalism Beats

Beat-specific workflows and reference information for different journalism categories.

## Politics Beat

**Focus:** Policy, elections, government, legislation, political campaigns

**Trusted Sources:**
- Official government websites (congress.gov, parliament.uk, etc.)
- Voting records and official transcripts
- Verified political news organizations
- Official campaign sources

**Key Questions:**
- What policy is being proposed or changed?
- Who are the key players and stakeholders?
- What is the timeline for implementation?
- What are the arguments for and against?

**Common Pitfalls:**
- Taking partisan statements as fact without verification
- Missing context about political processes
- Confusing speculation with reporting

**Output Format:** Inverted pyramid (most important info first), neutral tone, multiple viewpoints represented

## Technology Beat

**Focus:** Tech industry, startups, AI, software, hardware, product launches

**Trusted Sources:**
- TechCrunch, Ars Technica, The Verge
- Official company blogs and press releases
- SEC filings (for public companies)
- Technical documentation and repositories

**Key Questions:**
- What problem does this technology solve?
- Who is the target market?
- What are the technical specifications?
- What are the privacy/security implications?
- Who are the competitors?

**Common Pitfalls:**
- Press release regurgitation (marketing as news)
- Overhyping capabilities without verification
- Missing technical accuracy for complexity

**Output Format:** Feature-style, technical accuracy balanced with accessibility, include real-world use cases

---

## Business Beat

**Focus:** Markets, companies, economy, finance, corporate developments

**Trusted Sources:**
- Bloomberg, Reuters, Financial Times
- SEC filings (10-K, 10-Q, 8-K)
- Earnings call transcripts
- Company press releases (with skepticism)

**Key Questions:**
- What are the financial numbers?
- What is the market impact?
- Who are the key executives?
- What are the risks and opportunities?
- How does this compare to competitors?

**Common Pitfalls:**
- Forward-looking statements as predictions
- Missing financial context
- Company PR without verification

**Output Format:** Business-focused, include numbers and metrics, context about market implications

---

## Sports Beat

**Focus:** Games, athletes, teams, statistics, analysis

**Trusted Sources:**
- Official league/score sources
- Official team/athlete statements
- Verified statistics databases
- Reputable sports journalism outlets

**Key Questions:**
- What was the score and key plays?
- What were the statistics?
- What are the implications for standings/playoffs?
- What did coaches/players say?

**Common Pitfalls:**
- Rumors as fact
- Overinterpreting single games
- Missing context about longer-term trends

**Output Format:** Action-oriented, include key stats, quotes from participants

---

## Entertainment Beat

**Focus:** Movies, TV, streaming, celebrities, gaming, pop culture

**Trusted Sources:**
- Industry publications (Variety, Hollywood Reporter)
- Official studio/streaming service statements
- Verified social media accounts
- Reputable reviews and criticism

**Key Questions:**
- What is being released or announced?
- Who are the key people involved?
- What are the early reactions or reviews?
- What are the business implications?

**Common Pitfalls:**
- Gossip as news
- Blind items as fact
- Missing cultural context

**Output Format:** Engaging but factual, separate news from opinion/reviews
