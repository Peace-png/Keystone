## Cognitive Market Mechanics

### Trading Operations

```
BUY (Acquire Belief):
├─ Due diligence: Evidence gathering
├─ Risk assessment: What if I'm wrong?
├─ Position sizing: Confidence allocation
└─ Portfolio impact: How does this change my worldview?

SELL (Release Belief):
├─ Loss recognition: I was wrong
├─ Opportunity cost: What else could I hold?
├─ Tax implications: Cognitive cost of change
└─ Portfolio rebalancing: What fills the void?

HOLD (Maintain Position):
├─ Stop loss: At what point do I abandon?
├─ Take profit: When is this "done"?
├─ Monitoring: New evidence tracking
└─ Hedging: What covers my downside?
```

## Market Failure Modes

### Cognitive Biases as Market Failures

| Bias | Market Analog | Description |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| **Confirmation Bias** | Insider trading | Trading on non-public information (your existing beliefs) |
| **Sunk Cost Fallacy** | Holding losing position | Refusing to sell because of past investment |
| **Dunning-Kruger** | Irrational exuberance | Overconfidence in ability to predict |
| **Groupthink** | Market bubble | Consensus without critical evaluation |
| **Availability Heuristic** | Recency bias | Overweighting recent data |
| **Anchoring** | Price stickiness | First information dominates |

### Recovery Protocols

```
MARKET CRASH (Cognitive Collapse):
├─ Trigger: Overwhelming evidence contradicts portfolio
├─ Symptoms: Confusion, denial, anger, bargaining
├─ Recovery: Accept loss, rebalance, rebuild from support
└─ Prevention: Diversification (beliefs), stop losses (skepticism)

BUBBLE (Overconfidence):
├─ Trigger: Extended winning streak, validation echo chamber
├─ Symptoms: Risk blindness, certainty, dismissal of critics
├─ Recovery: Skeptic injection, bridge to alternative views
└─ Prevention: Regular skeptic review, humbling cascade

ILLIQUIDITY (Analysis Paralysis):
├─ Trigger: Too many positions, insufficient confidence
├─ Symptoms: Can't decide, overthinking, missed opportunities
├─ Recovery: Pragmatist veto (pick something), micro focus (one thing)
└─ Prevention: Position limits, decision deadlines
```

## Market Thinking Heuristics

### Decision Questions

1. **What's my position?** (What do I believe?)
2. **What's the evidence?** (Market data)
3. **What's the downside?** (Risk assessment)
4. **What's the upside?** (Opportunity)
5. **When do I exit?** (Stop loss)
6. **What am I missing?** (Skeptic review)

### Portfolio Health Checks

- **Diversification**: Do I have bets across multiple outcomes?
- **Correlation**: Are my beliefs independent or redundant?
- **Concentration**: Am I overexposed to one position?
- **Liquidity**: Can I change my mind if needed?
- **Performance**: Have my beliefs been winning or losing?
